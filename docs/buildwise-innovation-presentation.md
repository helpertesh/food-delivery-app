# Buildwise Innovation — Pitch Deck & Speaker Guide

**Project:** Food delivery platform (web app with customer, admin, and payment flows)  
**Use this file as:** slide outline + what to say + demo checklist.

**Suggested total time:** 5–7 minutes pitch + 2–3 minutes Q&A (adjust to your slot).

---

## Slide 0 — Title (5 seconds)

**On screen:** Product name, team names, one-line tagline.

**Say:**  
“We built a food delivery experience designed for how people actually pay and order in Kenya—fast checkout, clear tracking, and tools for restaurants to run the business—not just list menus.”

---

## 1. The problem — hook them in

**On screen (bullets, not paragraphs):**

- Ordering food should take **minutes**, not back-and-forth calls or confusion about payment.
- Many users rely on **mobile money (M-Pesa)**, not cards—generic global apps often feel mismatched.
- Restaurants need **simple operations**: menu, orders, pricing—without enterprise complexity.

**What judges should feel:**  
“This is real friction I recognize.”

**Optional opening line (pick one):**

- “How many times have you abandoned an order because payment or trust wasn’t clear?”
- “In markets where M-Pesa is the default, ‘card-first’ checkout is not innovation—it’s friction.”

**Speaker note:**  
Keep it to **one story** (busy lunch, campus, family dinner). Then zoom out to the three bullets.

---

## 2. The solution — simple and clear

**On screen:** One diagram or 3 icons: **Browse → Pay (M-Pesa) → Track**.

**Say in one breath:**  
“We give customers a clean web app to browse the menu, pay with **M-Pesa STK Push**, and follow their order status end-to-end. Restaurants use an **admin** view to manage food, pricing, and operations. We layered in **loyalty points** so repeat customers are rewarded without extra apps.”

**If you show the product, point to:**

- Menu, cart, checkout with M-Pesa.
- Order timeline / status (preparing → on the way → delivered).
- Admin: menu and order management (high level—no need every button).

**Speaker note:**  
If they only remember **three words**, they should be: **pay, track, operate**.

---

## 3. How we’re different — our edge

**On screen:** “Local-first + operational + intelligent assist”

| Edge | What it means (plain language) |
|------|--------------------------------|
| **M-Pesa-native checkout** | Meets users where they pay; not an afterthought. |
| **Full loop, not a mockup** | Customer journey + admin + persistence + payments path—not just a UI slide. |
| **AI assist (optional)** | In-app help and **personalized meal suggestions** powered by AI when API keys are configured; sensible fallback without keys so the demo always works. |
| **Loyalty built in** | Points and redemptions tied to real orders—encourages retention. |

**Say:**  
“Global aggregators optimize for their markets. We optimize for **local payment behavior**, **operator workflows**, and **smarter discovery** without forcing everyone onto card wallets.”

**Speaker note:**  
Don’t trash competitors. Contrast on **fit**, not insults.

---

## 4. Value — what users and businesses gain

**On screen:** Short value table.

| Who | Value |
|-----|--------|
| **Customers** | Saves time—order and pay in one flow; less uncertainty; rewards for coming back. |
| **Restaurants / admins** | Saves time—menu and orders in one place; fewer manual payment reconciliations when STK is integrated. |
| **Platform** | Data on orders and loyalty enables better recommendations and future analytics. |

**One line to land:**  
“We reduce **time-to-order**, **payment friction**, and **operational chaos** for small and mid-sized food businesses.”

---

## 5. Scale — how this grows

**On screen:** A simple ladder: **City → Region → Multi-tenant SaaS**.

**Say:**

1. **Technical:** Backend is structured for **more restaurants, more locations, and higher order volume**; database-backed, API-driven—ready to split services or add caching as load grows.
2. **Geographic:** Same stack extends to new cities; M-Pesa remains the payment backbone; other mobile money or cards can be added as modules.
3. **Business:** Move from **single-brand pilot** to **multi-tenant** (each restaurant or chain as a tenant) with shared infrastructure.
4. **Partnerships:** Cloud kitchen brands, campus programs, corporate lunch programs—each is a **distribution channel**, not a rewrite.

**Speaker note:**  
Judges want to hear **“this isn’t a school project that dies after demo day.”** Emphasize **modular payments**, **admin multi-store path**, and **repeat usage** (loyalty).

---

## 6. Business model — how we make money

**On screen:** Pick the mix that matches your real plan (be honest in Q&A).

| Stream | How it works |
|--------|----------------|
| **Commission per order** | % of GMV (e.g. 10–20%)—common in marketplaces. |
| **SaaS subscription** | Monthly fee for admin, analytics, priority support. |
| **Delivery or service fee** | Small flat or distance-based fee per order (if you operate or partner on logistics). |
| **Promoted placement / deals** | Restaurants pay for visibility or featured slots on the menu. |

**Say:**  
“Our primary path is **commission on orders** plus **recurring SaaS** for restaurants that want branded ops, reports, and support. As we scale, **marketing placements** become incremental revenue without hurting core UX.”

**Speaker note:**  
If logistics are not built yet, say **“we partner with riders or existing fleets”**—credibility beats overselling.

---

## 7. Close — strong final statement

**On screen:** One sentence only (large type).

**Example closers (choose one and own it):**

- “We’re not importing Silicon Valley checkout—we’re **building the default food ordering layer for M-Pesa-first customers and the kitchens that serve them.**”
- “**Local payment, clear operations, loyal customers**—that’s how we win markets the giants treat as secondary.”
- “Tomorrow’s default food app in this region won’t be the one with the most features—it will be the one that **removes friction at pay time and keeps restaurants in control.**”

**Then:**  
“Thank you—we’d love your questions.”

---

## Demo flow (2 minutes, if allowed)

1. **Customer:** Log in → open menu → add to cart → **M-Pesa** checkout (or simulation—say so clearly).  
2. **Tracking:** Show order status / timeline.  
3. **Extra wow (optional):** AI chat or “recommended for you” if configured.  
4. **Admin:** Quick peek—menu item or order list (proves **two-sided** system).

**Backup:** Screenshots or a short screen recording if live demo risk is high.

---

## Q&A — likely questions & tight answers

| Question | Answer direction |
|----------|------------------|
| “Is M-Pesa live?” | “We integrated STK Push; in demo we use [sandbox/simulation] and document path to production keys.” |
| “How do you get restaurants?” | “Pilot with 2–5 kitchens, prove order volume, then expand by neighborhood or campus.” |
| “What about delivery?” | “Phase 1: partner riders or store pickup; phase 2: in-app rider assignment and tracking.” |
| “Why web not app?” | “Faster iteration, no app store gate, works on any phone—PWA or native wrapper comes next.” |
| “AI privacy?” | “We send minimal menu/order context to the model; we can add retention policies and on-device options later.” |

- [ ] One **problem** story rehearsed out loud.  
- [ ] **Demo path** works offline or on hotspot backup.  
- [ ] **Numbers** ready (even estimates): order time saved, target commission %, pilot timeline.  
- [ ] **Team roles** clear (who speaks, who drives demo).  
- [ ] **Closing line** memorized verbatim.
